## General Questions

### 1. **Q: What is SecureBase?**
**A:** SecureBase is an executive-grade governance intelligence platform that turns fragmented infrastructure, security, and compliance telemetry into clear, defensible risk insight for boards, executive leadership, risk committees, and audit stakeholders.

### 2. **Q: Who is SecureBase for?**
**A:** SecureBase is designed for regulated and oversight-heavy organizations, including fintech firms, healthcare companies, government contractors, SaaS platforms with audit obligations, and institutions that need stronger executive visibility into operational risk and compliance posture.

### 3. **Q: What problem does SecureBase solve?**
**A:** Most organizations struggle with fragmented evidence, low-context alerts, and reporting that is too technical for executive stakeholders. SecureBase consolidates those signals into governance-ready intelligence that supports remediation prioritization, board review, audit defensibility, and regulatory dialogue.

### 4. **Q: How is SecureBase different from a DIY AWS setup?**
**A:** A DIY setup can create infrastructure, but it rarely produces leadership-ready governance visibility on its own. SecureBase combines infrastructure foundations, evidence integrity, compliance posture monitoring, and executive reporting into a single operating model.

### 5. **Q: How is SecureBase different from Terraform Cloud or infrastructure tooling?**
**A:** Terraform and similar tools help teams provision and manage infrastructure. SecureBase sits above raw provisioning by translating technical state into governance intelligence, executive visibility, and audit-supporting evidence workflows.

### 6. **Q: How is SecureBase different from monitoring platforms?**
**A:** Traditional monitoring tools surface technical signals. SecureBase organizes those signals into executive risk domains such as privacy exposure, privileged access risk, regulatory posture, vendor risk, and operational resilience.

### 7. **Q: What governance domains does SecureBase focus on?**
**A:** SecureBase is oriented around five strategic exposure domains: Data Sovereignty & Privacy Risk, Access Governance & Privileged Identity Exposure, Regulatory Compliance Posture, Vendor & Third-Party Risk, and Operational Resilience & Control Integrity.

### 8. **Q: Is there an executive briefing or pilot?**
**A:** Yes. We offer an executive access program with governance-focused briefings, white-glove onboarding, and limited pilot pricing for qualified organizations.

## Technical & Platform Questions

### 9. **Q: Does SecureBase still include AWS infrastructure foundations?**
**A:** Yes. SecureBase still builds on strong AWS governance and landing-zone foundations, including multi-account structure, centralized identity, logging, and baseline control enforcement. The difference is that these foundations are now presented through a governance and executive-risk lens.

### 10. **Q: What data does SecureBase use?**
**A:** SecureBase aggregates infrastructure telemetry, identity and access signals, compliance evidence, audit artifacts, and operational control data to produce a more coherent view of institutional risk posture.

### 11. **Q: How does SecureBase preserve evidentiary integrity?**
**A:** SecureBase uses an integrity loop architecture: evidence is collected, hashed, vaulted, digitally signed, and verified before it is rendered in the production governance portal. This supports defensible audit and attestation workflows.

### 12. **Q: What does the executive portal show?**
**A:** The executive portal emphasizes board-ready views such as enterprise risk heatmaps, framework posture indicators, remediation trajectory, exposure concentration, and strategic risk summaries rather than raw low-level telemetry.

### 13. **Q: Can technical teams still use SecureBase?**
**A:** Absolutely. SecureBase remains useful to technical and security teams, but its core value is helping those teams translate technical reality into institutional decision support for leadership.

### 14. **Q: Can I export my infrastructure configuration or evidence artifacts?**
**A:** Yes. SecureBase supports export of Terraform-based configuration and preserves auditable evidence artifacts that can support transition planning, audit preparation, and internal governance review.

## Compliance & Risk Questions

### 15. **Q: Does SecureBase make us compliant?**
**A:** No platform alone can guarantee compliance. SecureBase strengthens your control environment, evidence integrity, and reporting posture so your organization can approach audits, diligence reviews, and regulator conversations with greater defensibility and clarity.

### 16. **Q: Which frameworks does SecureBase support?**
**A:** SecureBase supports governance visibility across major regulated frameworks including SOC 2, HIPAA, PCI DSS, SOX, and FedRAMP-informed environments.

### 17. **Q: Can SecureBase support board reporting?**
**A:** Yes. SecureBase is intentionally designed to support board leadership, audit committees, executive risk reviews, and regulator-facing conversations through concise, visual, high-context governance reporting.

### 18. **Q: How does SecureBase help during audits?**
**A:** SecureBase helps teams produce stronger audit narratives by preserving immutable evidence, time-bound governance snapshots, and historical posture reconstruction. This improves readiness, reduces scrambling, and supports executive attestation.

### 19. **Q: What about HIPAA or healthcare-specific requirements?**
**A:** SecureBase supports privacy and security governance for healthcare operators through HIPAA-oriented controls, evidentiary retention, BAA support in applicable tiers, and executive views into privacy and resilience exposure.

### 20. **Q: What about FedRAMP or government oversight?**
**A:** SecureBase supports organizations operating in FedRAMP-informed and NIST-oriented environments by emphasizing high-assurance evidence handling, oversight cadence, and regulator-conscious governance reporting.
